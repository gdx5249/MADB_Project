Challenges & Future Outlook

Despite its economic significance, golf has faced participation challenges. Between 2002 and 2016, the number of U.S. golfers declined by 24%, driven by time constraints and changing leisure preferences. Traditional 18-hole rounds often require 4 hours or more, making it difficult for busy individuals to participate.

However, the landscape is evolving. The rise of alternative formats like Topgolf and the sport‚Äôs increasing demographic diversity signal a positive trajectory. Golf‚Äôs adaptability ensures its ongoing relevance in modern society.

Future Trends

  • Younger and more diverse player base
