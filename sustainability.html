Evolution of Sustainable Golf Course Design

1960s

Early Awareness

Rachel Carson's groundbreaking book, "Silent Spring", has sparked a global environmental movement. Not only did it reveal the dangers of chemical pollution, but it also made some golf practitioners aware of the potential environmental problems of chemical use and began to try natural methods to regulate lawns.

Learn More
1980s

Pre-Conservation

The United States Golf Association (USGA) gradually developed the "Environmental Responsibility Guidelines" in the late 1980s, promoting structural thinking on water resources and conservation management, laying the foundation for environmental transformation in the 1990s

Learn More
2005

Eco-Certification Movement

At a critical time when the global environment is facing challenges, an increasing number of foundations are committed to golf and sustainable development actions, and have a positive impact on the environment and communities around the world. The GEO Foundation for Sustainable Golf has carried out a series of closely related work.

Learn More
2025

The Invention of AESIS System

Researchers have developed an agro-environmental sustainability information system (AESIS) by utilizing a set of indicators suitable for evaluating different dimensions of sustainability (physical, ecological, production, and social), which helps to evaluate the agricultural environment of golf courses correctly.
